Team Yell blocks your path!

A brief criticism about complacency in the most recent Pokémon games

27th February 2020 | Share: Facebook · Twitter

500 words / 2 minute read

I've almost finished Pokémon Sword, and I'm really enjoying it. Obviously. It's a Pokémon game! It will never not be fun to raise your team, give them the best moves, take on the Gym Challenge, and go all out. And there's always so much variety in terms of Pokémon, moves, and abilities, that (even if game progression is painfully linear) you never get bored.

However.

I want to draw up a very specific comparison between the latest generation of Pokémon games, and the very first generation of Pokémon games, to imply a broader point about the complacency that over time has forced its way into the franchise. (This is going to come across as a very minor gripe. And it is! It's one of many very minor gripes in fact.) Here it is: a list of every gym in Pokémon Red and Blue, and every stadium in Pokémon Sword and Shield, and the obstacles in the way of reaching them.

Pokémon Red and Blue (1998)
What stops me from going to...?

Pewter Gym

You need to get your Starter Pokémon and take on the Gym Challenge!

Cerulean Gym

A youngster blocks your path, until you beat Pewter Gym.

Vermilion Gym

You need Cut from S.S. Anne, and must beat Cerulean Gym so as to be allowed to use it.

Celadon Gym

You need Flash from Route 2 so that you can navigate through the Rock Tunnel.

Fuchsia Gym

You need a Poké Flute to wake up Snorlax, and a bike to navigate Cycling Road.

Saffron Gym

You need to persuade thirsty gatehouse guards to let you through by offering them a drink, and then you need to take down Team Rocket.

Cinnabar Gym

You need Surf from the Safari Zone, and must beat Fuchsia Gym so as to be allowed to use it.

Viridian Gym

You must beat all other gyms.

Pokémon Sword and Shield (2019)
What stops me from going to...?

Turffield Stadium

You need to get your Starter Pokémon and take on the Gym Challenge!

Hulbury Stadium

Team Yell blocks your path, until you beat Turffield Stadium.

Motostoke Stadium

Team Yell blocks your path, until you beat Hulbury Stadium.

Stow-on-Side Stadium

Team Yell blocks your path, until you beat Motostoke Stadium.

Ballonlea Stadium

Team Yell blocks your path, until you beat Stow-on-Side Stadium.

Circhester Stadium

Can't remember. But, if I were a betting man...

Spikemuth Stadium

Team Yell terrorises a guy that wants to upgrade your bike, and you need a bike upgrade to progress. They move on after you beat Circhester Stadium.

Hammerlocke Stadium

You must beat all other gyms.
